Bio

My name is Xiaohe He—and in Chinese, it sounds exactly like “笑呵呵” (xiào hē hē), which means “always laughing and cheerful.” It perfectly reflects my positive, optimistic approach to life.

I completed my PhD in June 2025 from the University of Chinese Academy of Sciences, majoring in Communication and Information Systems. I earned my bachelor’s degree from Harbin Institute of Technology back in 2015. My doctoral research focused on “Agile Satellite Mission Planning Using Deep Reinforcement Learning.” In simpler terms: modern agile satellites can maneuver quickly to point at different locations on Earth, and my work involved teaching AI to optimally schedule their imaging tasks—a deeply complex puzzle that kept me up many nights!

Beyond academics, I’m a big fan of board games and sports. In my spare time, I love playing Chinese chess (Xiangqi) and badminton.

My recent work has focused on Large Language Models and Agentic RL.
